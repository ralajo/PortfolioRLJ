Hurri-Safe

How migh we utilize exsisting IoT to make Hurricane Rescue more efficient?



Executive Summary

When hurricanes come, people need to leave, but evacuation efforts always leave people behind, they cannot make contact with and rescue everyone in the current system.

Currently, with the climate crisis at hand, the frequency and intensity of these storms are increasing and in turn the need to be able to evacuate people from such conditions with ease grows.

From outside research, interviews with crisis management personnel, and discussions with engineers, we created Hurrisafe, a module that tracks people by heat and creates a local network to enable communication from officials to those left on the ground and to enable those who are stuck to be tracked by their body heat.

Problem

Hurricane rescue is currently extremely inefficient, with many left behind and left confused about where their appropriate shelter. This can be absolutely devastating to communities. A large rout of this pain comes from an inability to communicate to those who need help and are providing it, as hurricanes take out natural power lines. With this project, we tried to figure out if there was a way to make Hurricane Rescue more efficient with technology.

Methods

The begging of answering this question was building a foundation on the topic of Hurricanes through historical records, state preparedness manuals, and interviews with those in charge. From there we looked more into technology to learn what types of sensors we could use to make our idea work. Finally, we began to make our video, and present our solution, iterating through storyboards scripts and videos to create something that professional to explain what's happening in made the concept of a local area network and a shelter plan really quickly and persuasively in a very short time frame.

Tactics
Interview, Storyboarding (Both Design & Film Style), Technical Research, Critique

Solution

Our solution, Hurri-Safe is a tiny module that can be dropped off of planes and will dig into the ground using its claes. It is heavy enough weight to support itself in Hurricanes situations after later blows.

Once it lands, it creates a local area network which it will allow for those within the area to receive alerts on their phones from officials about evacuation times, and shelter directions & capacity updates. Additionally, the infrared sensor on the module can be used to scan the area for heat, which is a fairly good representation of people enabling officials to better optimize their rescue routes and increase the time when they can carry out rescues to include dark hours.

In summary, by creating a local area network and scanning for heat HurriSafe can improve the efficiency and effectiveness of all important hurricane rescue missions
